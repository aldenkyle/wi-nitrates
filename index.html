CANCER IN THE WATER?:

Exploring the connection between nitrate concentrations and cacner rates in Wisconsin

In order to understand the relationship between nitrate concentrations from well measurements and cancer rates we will first estimate the nitrate concentration in each census tract by interpolating known nitrate concentration measurements and aggregating those results into each census tract. Next we will run a linear regression to understand the relationship between the assessed nitrate concentration and cancer rate in each census tract. Users must input their own exponent of distance as there is no theory to say exactly what that value should be. According to esri the distance exponent, "controls the significance of surrounding points on the interpolated value. A higher power results in less influence from distant points. It can be any real number greater than 0, but the most reasonable results will be obtained using values from 0.5 to 3."
